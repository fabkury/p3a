# Museum-Mode Demo Video — Production Brief

*Companion to `museum-outreach-plan.md`. Drafted 2026-05-16.*

The single most leveraged Tier 1 asset: a 30-second demo video that
shows p3a playing real artwork from the five supported museums (AIC,
Rijksmuseum, V&A, Wellcome Collection, SMK). Drops into every
Tier 1 venue, the README's Museums section, and the social-launch wave.

This document has two audiences:

1. **You (the user)** — what raw footage to capture *before* hiring the
   editor, so the editor has something to work with.
2. **The Fiverr editor** — what to deliver, mood references, and
   don't-do items. The brief in Part B is self-contained and can be
   pasted directly into a Fiverr seller intake.

---

## Part A — Pre-flight: capture this footage yourself

Plan ~2–3 hours total over one or two evenings. Phone camera is fine
(modern iPhone / Pixel will out-resolve most Fiverr editors' workflows
anyway). Tripod mandatory — no handheld shake.

**Setup**
- Plain wall or matte background behind the device.
- Indirect lighting from one side; avoid overhead glare on the IPS
  panel.
- Phone in 4K at 30fps. Lock exposure and white balance after framing.
- Keep the device powered from a wall outlet — no battery surprises
  mid-shoot.

**Curate the playlist before shooting.** Make a 10–15 artwork rotation
that hits all five museums:

| Museum | Suggested examples (substitute freely) |
|---|---|
| AIC | A Vermeer, a Hopper, a Monet *Water Lilies* panel |
| Rijksmuseum | Vermeer *Milkmaid*, Rembrandt portrait, Saenredam interior |
| V&A | A Julia Margaret Cameron photograph, a William Morris design |
| Wellcome Collection | Vesalius anatomical plate, a botanical illustration |
| SMK | A Hammershøi interior, a Krøyer Skagen beach scene |

Avoid: anything dark/low-key (reads as a black screen on camera), busy
abstract pieces (don't sell the "real painting" promise as quickly).

**Shots to capture (~12 clips, 5–8 seconds each)**

1. **Hero pull-back.** Device on a desk or shelf, painting on screen,
   slow dolly-out from 8" to 24". Repeat twice with different
   paintings (a Rembrandt and a Vermeer work well).
2. **In-context.** Device sitting next to a stack of books / a mug /
   houseplant — the "tiny gallery on your desk" feel.
3. **Mid close-up of the screen.** Tight enough that the artwork fills
   frame; static, 8 seconds. Repeat for 3 different museums (lets the
   editor cut between them).
4. **Touch interaction.** A finger swiping or tapping — 2 takes,
   different paintings on screen.
5. **Boot / wake.** Plug-in or wake-from-sleep moment. The first frame
   of artwork appearing is a strong "it just works" beat.
6. **Side angle.** 3/4 view showing the device's thickness and bezel.
7. **Web flasher (optional, screen-recorded).** 5-second clip of the
   web flasher in a browser, button click → progress bar moving. If
   the editor uses it at all it'll be a 2-second cutaway.

**File handoff**
- Upload to a Dropbox / Google Drive folder, share read-only link.
- Include a one-line label per clip ("01_hero_pullback_vermeer.mp4")
  so the editor doesn't have to guess.

## Part B — Fiverr brief (paste this into the seller intake)

> **Project:** 30-second demo video for an open-source desktop art
> frame (p3a). Source: pre-shot 4K phone clips of the device displaying
> paintings from five major museums.
>
> **Deliverables (please quote for all of these):**
> - Master cut: 30 seconds, 16:9, 1080p H.264 MP4.
> - Vertical cut: 30 seconds, 9:16, 1080p — same edit reframed for
>   Bluesky / Mastodon / TikTok / Instagram Reels.
> - Two audio versions of each: with light background music
>   (royalty-free, your pick), and music-less (sound design / ambient
>   room tone only) for venues where music feels out of place
>   (museum-team emails, IIIF community).
>
> **Mood:** calm-tech / slow-gallery aesthetic. Think Apple product
> film *not* tech-bro highlight reel. Slow cuts (2–4 seconds), smooth
> dissolves between paintings, no whip-pans, no glitch effects, no
> on-screen text overlays except the closing card.
>
> **Structure (suggested, flexible):**
> - 0–4s: hero pull-back, device on a desk displaying a recognizable
>   painting.
> - 4–18s: three or four cross-fades between different paintings
>   filling the screen, each ID'd by a small lower-third for ~1s with
>   the museum name (e.g., "Rijksmuseum"). Lower-third in a clean
>   serif, lower-left.
> - 18–24s: in-context shot — device on a shelf next to everyday
>   objects.
> - 24–28s: brief touch-interaction beat.
> - 28–30s: closing card, white background:
>   `p3a — open-hardware art frame`
>   `github.com/fabkury/p3a`
>
> **Don't-do list:**
> - No AI-generated visuals or stock footage. The audience for this
>   video includes pixel-art and OpenGLAM communities that will notice
>   and roast.
> - No fast cuts / techno music / "epic" trailer pacing.
> - No "as seen on" logos.
> - No screen-glow simulation effects added in post — the real IPS
>   panel speaks for itself.
> - No on-screen text other than the closing card and museum
>   lower-thirds.
>
> **Music:** royalty-free, ambient / minimal piano / soft strings.
> No vocals. Examples for reference: any "Apple keynote ambient" track
> on Artlist or Musicbed. If unsure, please send 2 options to choose
> from before the final render.
>
> **Source material:** 4K phone clips, ~12 labeled files, ~2 GB total,
> delivered via a shared Dropbox / Drive folder.
>
> **Round count:** 1 round of revisions included (timing, music swap,
> color tweaks).
>
> **Timeline:** 5 business days from source-file delivery is fine.
>
> **Budget:** $75 baseline, willing to go to $100 for the right editor.
>
> **License:** Buyout — I need the right to use the final cuts on
> social media, GitHub, and editorial press kits indefinitely. Music
> license must cover commercial / open-source-project use.

## Sellers to look at

Filter Fiverr search by:

- **Level 2 or Top Rated.**
- **Sample work showing product / unboxing edits**, not wedding /
  travel reels.
- **English communication** at a level that handles "lower-third" and
  "cross-fade" without a glossary.
- Sort by **Best Selling** and read the last 10 reviews — look for
  reviews mentioning "calm pacing," "Apple-style," "clean."

Three search queries that surface the right pool:

1. `product demo video editor`
2. `apple-style product film`
3. `tech unboxing video edit`

Send the brief to 2–3 sellers as a "Get a Quote" message; pick the
fastest, clearest reply, not the cheapest.

## Acceptance checklist (review the delivered cuts against this)

- [ ] 30 seconds exactly (±0.5s).
- [ ] Both aspect ratios (16:9 and 9:16) delivered.
- [ ] Both audio variants (with-music and music-less) delivered.
- [ ] Each museum identified by a clean lower-third when its artwork
      appears.
- [ ] Closing card legible at thumbnail size.
- [ ] No AI-generated content anywhere in the cut.
- [ ] Color in the artwork panels looks faithful (not over-saturated
      or pushed into "vibrant product" territory).
- [ ] H.264 MP4 plays cleanly in Bluesky / Mastodon / Twitter previews.
- [ ] Music license documentation attached.
