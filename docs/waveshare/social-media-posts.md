## Hackaday.io / Hackster.io (project description — short version)

> **p3a — ESP32-P4 Pixel Art Player**
>
> p3a is an open-source animated pixel art player built on the Waveshare ESP32-P4-WIFI6-Touch-LCD-4B board. It leverages the board's 720x720 IPS display, capacitive touch, Wi-Fi 6, and SD card to deliver a smooth, connected pixel art experience.
>
> It plays trending GIFs from Giphy, curated artworks from Makapix Club (a pixel art social network), and local files from the SD card. Users control everything through the touchscreen or a web interface served directly by the device.
>
> Key technical highlights:
> - Triple-buffered frame rendering with VSYNC for stutter-free playback
> - SHA256-sharded artwork vault for efficient SD card caching
> - Automatic OTA updates for both main firmware and the ESP32-C6 co-processor
> - Captive portal for zero-config Wi-Fi setup
> - WebSocket-based real-time web UI
>
> GitHub: https://github.com/fabkury/p3a

## Tips for posting

1. **Always include a demo video or photo** — a short clip of p3a playing animated pixel art on the display is the most persuasive asset.
2. **Post on community platforms first** (Reddit, Hackaday, Hackster) — traction there strengthens the pitch to Waveshare.
3. **Tag Waveshare on X** — they monitor mentions and often repost community projects.
4. **Follow up** — if you don't hear back from the email within 1-2 weeks, reply to the same thread with a polite follow-up.
